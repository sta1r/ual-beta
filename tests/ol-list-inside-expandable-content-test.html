Postgraduate Scholarships

University of the Arts London is pleased to offer a number of scholarships to those wishing to study at Postgraduate level. These awards are funded by generous private donations, company donations, philanthropic charities and University funds.

These awards offer help, ranging from fee waivers to full support for a year or more. Please see the individual awards for details of application procedures and application forms.

The Vice-Chancellor's Scholarships

A number of Vice Chancellor's Scholarships are available for Home/EU and international Masters students across University of the Arts London, for courses starting in January 2014.

All taught Masters courses will be considered for scholarship funding.

Please note that these scholarships are ONLY available to applicants who will start a new University of the Arts London Postgraduate course in the 2013/14 academic year. These awards are not available to those continuing their existing UAL postgraduate studies in 2013/14.

The available awards are as follows:

  • A number of full fee waivers for Home/EU students. Both new applicants and UAL graduates will be considered.
  • A number of Vice-Chancellor’s Postgraduate Scholarships for international students, which will provide a full tuition fee waiver at the overseas rate.
  • A number of additional awards of £4000 towards fees will also be available to UAL students and graduates progressing from Undergraduate to Postgraduate study.

The Vice-Chancellor’s Scholarships, funded by the University will be awarded according to the applicant’s likelihood of achieving academic excellence. There will be no scholarship application forms. Scholarships will be awarded based on your course application, subsequent portfolio and interview. Recommendations will be made by Course Leaders or Programme Directors.

Vice-Chancellor's Scholarships Eligibility - All applications from Home/EU/overseas are subject to the same scholarship selection process. Please read this section carefully. Please note there are NO application forms for these scholarships. 

Applicants must:

  • Have an offer of a place and take up their place in the 2013/14 academic year
  • Submit their course application by the end of the first week of October. We guarantee we will process your application in time for you to be considered for a scholarship.
  • Demonstrate academic excellence, which will be assessed based on:
  1. The quality of the applicant's work
  2. The clarity of the applicant's descriptions and insights into their work and subject
  3. The applicant's potential for future development and how their practice could develop as a result of the course
  4. The applicant's commitment to their practice, including their broader experience (shows, prizes, internships, community work, other activities) and long term commitment to professional practice

Further discount for UAL alumni

All University of the Arts London graduating undergraduate students who are progressing onto a postgraduate course are entitled to a further £1000 fee discount in addition to the progression bursary. More details are available on the Tuition Fees page.

Any questions? Please email funding@arts.ac.uk.

The Mead Scholarships & Fellowships

The Mead Scholarships and Fellowships are one of the most prestigious student and graduate support programmes at the University, designed to support University of the Arts London students and recent graduates in the pursuit of excellence through the development of their creative practice.

  • The Mead Scholarships and Fellowships are for talented students and graduates who have demonstrated outstanding ideas and excellence in practice across a wide range of creative sectors, or the use of creative solutions in other sectors.
  • The Mead Scholarship will provide up to three awards of up to £2500 each for Undergraduate students who have already exhibited a high level of creative potential and excellence in practice, and have one further year of study at UAL following receipt of the grant, thus allowing them the summer period before entering their final year of study to develop a body of research and/or practice at a crucial time.
  • The Mead Fellowship will provide up to two awards of up to £10,000 each to new and recent graduates, allowing them the time and flexibility to develop a body of research and/or practice after their graduation.

The Mead Programme is managed by SEE (Student Enterprise and Employability) and generously supported by philanthropist Scott Mead, for an initial five years, beginning 2012/13. Scott Mead is a fine art photographer, philanthropist and investor based in London for over 20 years. From his base in London, many of his current business and philanthropic activities take him to the United States and Asia on a regular basis.

Scott's photographs have been exhibited and sold in the UK, Europe and USA and are held in various private and institutional collections. He is involved with a number of major institutions in education, healthcare and the arts through the Mead Family Foundation, of which he is Founder and Chairman. 

He has had over 30 years of experience in the financial services sector, including as a partner and managing director of Goldman Sachs, which he left in 2003, and an investor and director with start-up businesses in media and technology.

For more information, including how to apply, please visit: see.arts.ac.uk/mead

The South Square Trust Scholarships

The South Square Trust offers three Scholarships for students studying on the Full Time MA Fine Art course at Central Saint Martins College of Arts and Design at the Archway Campus, previously the Byam Shaw School of Art.

  • The award is valued at £3000 maintenance per year.
  • Award open to UK nationals only.
  • Award based on academic merit and financial need.

The South Square Trust offers three Scholarships for students studying on the MA Fine Art course at Central Saint Martins.

The South Square Trust assists individuals wishing to study in the Fine and Applied Arts. For many years the Trust supported Fine Art students at the Byam Shaw School of Art, and this historic connection is continued through the Trust’s support of MA Fine Art students at the Archway campus, where the Byam Shaw School was based. 

The scholarships are each worth £3,000 per year over two years and will be a contribution towards maintenance for a student accepted on the Full-time MA Fine Art course at Central Saint Martins College of Arts and Design. Applications are open to UK students only who will benefit from postgraduate studies to realise their full potential and who are unable to finance themselves through their studies.

The scholarships will be awarded according to financial need and academic merit.

To be eligible for The South Square Trust Scholarship, applicants must fit all three of the following criteria:

  • A UK National
  • Accepted for a full-time Masters Degree in Fine Art at Central Saint Martins, commencing September 2013.
  • A graduate, having already gained a first or 2:1 in a Bachelors Degree from a Higher Education institution, or an HE accredited Further Education institution.

OR

(for applicants without a bachelor’s degree)

  • Able to demonstrate substantial equivalent and applicable experience.
